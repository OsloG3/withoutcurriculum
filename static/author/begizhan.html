Akhmedov Begizhan is a writer-educator, historian, and public figure from Kyrgyzstan. A researcher of Sufi doctrine and an expert-analyst on issues of national security and counter-terrorism, he holds the rank of reserve colonel. He is the author of more than ten books and over 300 articles.

He is a member of the Writers’ Unions of the Kyrgyz Republic, Uzbekistan, and the Republic of Azerbaijan, as well as an honorary professor at Babur State University and the International Kuwait University. His literary works have been translated into more than ten languages.

Among his best-known books is Derbish (“Wanderer”), which received the nomination “Best Book of 2020.”
Another important correction concerns one of his short stories: the correct title is “Gratitude”, while “Shadow of the Sentence” refers specifically to the first part of that work.
