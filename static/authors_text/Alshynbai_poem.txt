The Horse is My Only Companion

 I Dismounting Poem

 I am a poem etched into the hoofprints of a racing horse,
Pursuing the distant horizon where the sun meets the land.
Time’s ceaseless march has weathered me, Through scorching summers and piercing winter chills.

 The horse remained my one true companion,
Its warmth fortifying my very being.
I was the melody woven into the wolf’s mournful howl,
Drifting toward the moon in the silver glow of dusk,
A song that resonated through the kobyz.

 With the first light of dawn,
I soared across the endless steppe.
Perhaps my ancestors rode celestial steeds,
For my spirit neighs like a horse and sings through the kobyz.

 Only the vast steppe could truly hear our voices, Only the murmuring forests knew our song.
 The thunder of hooves carried me far and wide, And I vowed to protect their legacy.

 From the rugged strands of a horse’s mane,
A sacred banner of nine braided tails was born. And in the midnight rider’s solemn tune,
 A lingering ember of sorrow still glows within me.

 II Horses Beneath the Earth

 Memories of the past stir within me,
Unraveling echoes of forgotten times.
The horses resting deep in Berel—
Why do they linger in my thoughts?

 I see restless steeds before my eyes,
My heart gallops like a tethered horse.
Once, they roamed the dawn-lit plains,
Now their brittle bones lie scattered in the dust.

 Their chests once embraced the open wind, Silver bits clashing between their teeth.
Under which great khan did they charge into battle,
Their flowing manes glinting like sharpened blades?

 I mourn the horses buried beneath the soil,
As the earth sighs, yearning for their thunder.
On which vast steppe did they last run wild,
Their manes unfurling like banners in the wind?

 Ancient tombs worn by storms and time, Forgotten graves crumbling into dust,
Cities swallowed by shifting sands,
 burial mounds lost to drought…

And my horses, too,
rest beneath the earth.
Now, storm clouds gather above,
Lightning rips across the sky with fury.

I gaze upon the endless steppe,
Hearing phantom hooves,
 as if they rise from their graves.

 The land is a locked chest,
sealed with time, Beneath it,
 my history cries like an abandoned foal.

When will you return,
your voices ringing once more,
 O horses buried in Berel?

 III The present

 There was no room for sorrow back then,
 I was as full of life as the spring’s rushing waters. At the age of eight,
The black colt became my faithful companion.

 The mornings in the village were sweet,
And the evenings were grand, endless, and rich. The creature was wild,
Ready to tumble into the grave if provoked.

 From its foalhood days,
We pushed it to its limits.
It was always full of mischief,
And we became inseparable, bonded for life.

 During the bitter cold of January and February,
 I would keep it warm, protecting it from the freezing chill.
When danger approached,
I would cry out in grief for it.

 In moments of battle,
 I had no fear.
When it sprang into action,
It was like a bullet shot from a rifle.

 Stubborn and wild,
 Its spirit was fiery like glowing embers.
When it charged forward,
Others called it the leader of the herd.

 I never compared it to any other steed,
I kept my longing hidden deep within.
In the fields and during my daily tasks,
I always thought of the black colt.

 Fate, like a bird, carried us away,
Leaving my faithful steed behind.
The year the black colt grew strong,
I left for the city to pursue my studies.

 It felt like I was leaving behind something precious,
And many memories have faded with time.
Its eyes, so innocent like a young fawn’s,
 Would fill with tears when it called out in pain.

 The field that was once silent and still,
Now brings a smile to my face when I recall my return.
The black colt, raised from a foal,
Do you still remember the way home?

 The scent of its mane still lingers in my senses, And the longing for it never leaves my heart.
This song is filled with all my sorrow,
The cherished memory that I’ve kept close to my soul…

 IV The Song of the Horse’s Head

 My thoughts are tossed like a fish in a storm, Formed by ancient beliefs and customs.
Atop the dark tree, still hanging,
 Is the head of the horse slaughtered for my father’s meal.

 The first grief took root in my heart,
As many walked toward the grave.
Riding my father’s black steed,
I glanced back at that moment.

 In my song, echoes of that time linger,
A sorrow shaped by heavy destiny.
The animal’s small eyes, filled with tears,
 Let out a mournful neigh, shattering the stillness.

 The earth quietly took my sorrow,
Unaware of the hidden grave of youth.
 With my father’s black horse,
he departed for the afterlife,
 And I, the orphan, was left behind.

 Some nights,
I converse with my younger self,
Lost in the memories of days gone by.

Is the black horse resting in paradise,
 under my father’s care?
 Its scent still rises from its gray, curly mane.

 V The Horses of the Sky

 In the vast blue sky,
the blue-gray horses leave their trail,
As the Kokildy River meanders lazily below.
They charge forward, sweeping across countless ridges,
Galloping relentlessly until the sun sets.

 Their manes are as dark as the night,
 their coats as light as dawn,
 Treading gently on the grassy plains of blue.

 Like soft clouds in Koklen,
 A mighty herd of horses grazes by the river.
 Moonlight kisses the water’s surface,
While the beaver’s tail stirs in the crisp morning breeze.

Sunlight slides down their sleek bodies,
 As they glide through the sky,
just like the Kokildy.
 I carry within me the spirit of ancient Turkic beliefs,
My heart, a lost foal, drifts aimlessly.

 The horses neigh, their joy resonating across the land,
While the sky trembles with their thunderous gallop.
 Like a constellation of stars,
Koklen’s horses gallop,
The sweet scent of a mare’s milk fills the air.

When the sky’s vessel is full,
It spills its bounty upon the earth.
 As the night’s gaze gleams like a dark jewel,
 The horses’ breath fuels the sky’s blue glow.
 The first star to shine in Alakara,
Is the fiery gaze of the proud, untamed steeds.

 VI. The Water Stalion

 The vast blue sea,
the magnificent Caspian,
the mournful Aral,
A glance filled with fear, like a shy young girl’s eyes.

She plays with the waves,
Listening closely to their quiet,
secret whispers.
 As the yellow star fades in the cold night,
 Venus appears in the dark of the early morning.

The sea, touched by the flames’ soft glow,
Draws the horse from the stream to drink its water.
 The sky turns red, ablaze with fire,
I reach to touch the fiery ember,
 From the deep well, it rises, sharp as diamonds, Illuminating the dark night with its liquid light.

 The horse’s mane flows like silk,
A creature full of life, capturing the heart.
 When it bends its graceful neck,
It is more beautiful than any woman.

 The powerful hooves pound the earth in rhythm, The ground trembles under their weight.
In old times, horses were worth more than men, And every young man dreamed of owning such a steed.

 Many years have passed, too many to count, People rejoiced as they returned home safely.
 But the horses no longer run in the shallows,
Even the water has retreated from the shore.

 My heart is heavy with the sorrow of days gone by,
Oh, my song, don’t awaken old memories!
With the force of their hooves,
I wish to make the world spin beneath me.

 VII. The Return of the Horses

 Long ago, when a fierce storm raged overhead, And blood spilled in that cursed time.
In the ancient land of Turan, my people’s home, The white bones of the fallen scattered in the wind like dust.

 The winds of history howled like an unrelenting blizzard,
Spewing blood and foam over my brave warriors. The northern winds, harsh and unyielding,
Forced my horses into hiding, scattered in the storm’s fury.

 A heavy burden pressed upon my heart,
I clutched the reins, but my people were left to walk.
Thousands of my horses disappeared into the night,
Their ears like arrows, vanishing into the unknown.

 Fate’s cruel grip tightened around us,
 Many lives were lost in the vast wilderness.
 Dust clouds rose on the darkened roads,
 While sorrow and longing grew in the empty hills.

 The winds of the gods swept away the darkness, Revealing the true shades of the blue sky.
After the long night, the horses emerged,
And my heart always felt their thunderous return.

 Like a storm cloud about to burst,
The barren plains and towering mountains trembled.
 Their manes and tails blended together like a dark forest,
As my herd of horses emerged from the night.

 I witnessed the final glow of this grand era,
Would my grief fade if I embraced the flames? From the sand,
 I unearthed the treasures buried.
